MAGAZINE SPREAD

project summary

This project was the second project in the class IAT 233: Spatial Design in which students were required to art direct, design and photograph for a fashion magazine spread, beginning with a moodboard using Adobe software, particularly Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.

intention

For this project, I intended to create a magazine spread regarding geometric shapes and silhouettes in fashion. To suit and reflect the high contrast and elegance of the Bodoni font, I intended to develop a minimalistic, angular and sleek theme. Thus I dressed my model in geometric, boxy but flowy clothing with one main article that contrasted the rest of the outfit. I chose locations that had rougher textures and opposing colors to contrast the clothing. As well, I chose minimalistic backgrounds that contained interesting angles and shapes. I further intended to reflect high-fashion and elegant poses in my photographs. I then aimed to utilize those angles and shapes in my photographs in my composition in order to promote a strong underlying grid structure. I attempted to give the spread a generous amount of white space in order to support the theme of minimalism, and chose photos that contrasted for each spread.

challenges

As with all projects in this class, each project was to be completed within a week and thus, difficulty lay in creating an efficient and successful schedule. I also needed to contact a model and arrange a day to hold a photoshoot so it was crucial that I was prepared beforehand in order to allow for a smooth process.Thus, I carefully planned the upcoming week; I listed all the tasks that needed to be completed and the approximate time needed for their completion. As a result of creating a fast-paced and well-considered schedule of tasks to complete daily and following that schedule, I was able to complete the task in a timely manner while meeting my own expectations as to quality. There were also issues of competing visual weights on either side of the page which I was able to adequately combat, however in future iterations that would be an aspect I improve upon as my level of knowledge has increased.

result

From this project, I received praise from my professor for my design and ability to art direct and unify all aspects of the project, and thus prompted my interest in design and confidence as a designer. This project taught me key principles of graphic design that I continue to implement currently and on all future projects, such as composition and the importance of white space, as well as how to create a cohesive mood and tone. As this project required photography, I also learned the key elements involved in creating a particular tone through photographs and to correlate the mood of photographs to the mood of a design.
