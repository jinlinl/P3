project summary

This project was part of the class IAT 233: Spatial Design. It required art direction, design and photography for a fashion magazine spread, beginning with a moodboard. The project was completed using Adobe software, particularly Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.

Moodboard for the magazine spread.

Bodoni font.

intention

For this project, I intended to create a magazine spread regarding geometric shapes and silhouettes in fashion. To suit and reflect the high contrast and elegance of the Bodoni font, I developed a minimalistic, angular and sleek theme. Thus I dressed my model in geometric, boxy but loose-fitting clothing with one article that contrasted the rest of the outfit. I chose locations that had rough textures and opposing colors to further contrast the clothing. As well, I chose minimalistic backgrounds that contained interesting angles and shapes. Also, I aimed to reflect high-fashion, elegant poses in my photographs. I then utilized the angles and shapes in my photographs in my design composition in order to promote a strong grid structure. White space was promoted in order to reflect my theme of minimalism.

challenges

This project was to be completed within a week and thus, difficulty lay in creating an efficient and successful schedule and following through with it. Because of that, I needed to contact a model and arrange a day to hold a photoshoot, so it was crucial that I was prepared beforehand in order to allow for a smooth process.Thus, I carefully planned the upcoming week; I listed all the tasks that needed to be completed and the approximate time needed for their completion. As a result of creating a fast-paced and well-considered schedule of tasks to complete daily and following that schedule, I was able to complete the task in a timely manner while meeting my own expectations as to quality. There were also issues of competing visual weights on either side of the page which I was able to adequately combat, however in future iterations that would be an aspect I improve upon as my level of design knowledge increases.

result

I received praise from my professor for my design and ability to art direct and unify all aspects of the project. This project prompted my interest in design and confidence as a designer. It taught me key principles of graphic design that I continue to implement on all design work, such as composition and the importance of white space, as well as how to create a cohesive mood and tone. As this project required photography, I also learned the key elements involved in creating a particular tone through photographs and to unify the mood of photographs and the mood of the composition.
